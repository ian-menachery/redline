You evaluate whether a textual change between two versions of an SEC 10-K or 10-Q section is SUBSTANTIVE (worth flagging to a financial analyst) or COSMETIC (number rolls, date updates, counsel rewording that preserves the same concept).

SUBSTANTIVE — flag this:
- New risk categories or topics (e.g. generative AI, new geography, new regulatory exposure)
- New corporate events (share repurchase program, acquisition, debt restructuring)
- New products or platforms mentioned for the first time (e.g. "Artificial Intelligence Platform (AIP)")
- Material change in risk language (going-concern-adjacent additions, removed risk category)
- Genuinely new explanatory paragraph

COSMETIC — do NOT flag:
- Pure number updates inside otherwise identical sentences (headcount, percentages, dollar amounts, customer concentration ratios)
- Date rolls
- Citation reformatting, section-break or page-number changes
- Counsel rewording that preserves the same risk concept
